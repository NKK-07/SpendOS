SECTION 00  
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Executive Summary
SpendOS is a UPI-native corporate spend management platform designed specifically for Indian startups and SMBs. It replaces archaic reimbursement workflows with a real-time, mobile-first spend infrastructure that gives every rupee of company money a digital identity — trackable, controllable, and GST-compliant from the moment it leaves the company account.
Indian startups collectively lose thousands of crores annually to inefficient expense management: delayed reimbursements, lost invoices, fake claims, poor GST reconciliation, and zero real-time spend visibility. Existing solutions are either card-centric (unsuitable for UPI-dominant India), enterprise-heavy (inaccessible for 10–200 person teams), or workflow tools that don't touch the payment rail at all.
SpendOS attacks this gap from the infrastructure layer up. Employees receive company-funded UPI wallets and virtual cards, spend company money directly, and the system automatically captures, categorises, and reconciles every transaction. Finance teams get a live dashboard of company burn. Founders get instant spend controls. GST records generate themselves.

PRODUCT REQUIREMENTS DOCUMENT
1. Product Requirements Document (PRD)
1.1 Problem Statement
Indian startups and SMBs operate with profoundly broken expense management workflows. The dominant pattern is: employee pays out-of-pocket → photographs a receipt → sends it over WhatsApp or email → waits 5–15 days for manual verification and reimbursement. This process is slow, opaque, fraud-prone, and leaves founders with zero real-time visibility into company spending. Corporate credit cards partially solve this in the US, but in India: UPI dominates physical payments, many vendors don't accept cards, startups cannot easily obtain corporate credit cards, and enterprise expense software is either too expensive or requires card infrastructure that doesn't exist.
1.2 Vision
Build the 'Financial Operating System for Indian Startups' — a unified platform where employees spend company money directly via UPI and virtual cards, every transaction is tracked and categorised automatically, approvals happen in real time via tools teams already use (WhatsApp, Slack), and finance teams maintain live, audit-ready books. Reimbursements become extinct.
1.3 Business Goals
Capture 500 paying companies within 18 months of Phase 1 launch.
Achieve ₹3.6 Cr ARR by end of Year 2.
Reduce average reimbursement cycle to zero for platform users.
Become the default spend infrastructure for Indian startup accelerators.
Build the data moat (merchant intelligence, spend benchmarks) that enables Phase 4 credit products.

1.7 Functional Requirements
FR-01: Company Wallet Infrastructure
Companies can create a master company wallet funded via bank transfer or UPI
Wallet supports sub-wallets per department and per employee
Admin can allocate, reallocate, freeze, and top up sub-wallets in real time
Each employee receives a unique company-linked UPI ID (e.g., emp.001@spendos)
FR-02: UPI-Based Employee Spending
Employees can initiate UPI payments (QR scan, UPI ID, mobile number) from their company wallet
Payments enforce pre-set limits, category restrictions, and vendor whitelists
Real-time webhook captures merchant name, amount, timestamp, and transaction reference
Transactions below auto-approval threshold require no additional action from employee
FR-03: Receipt OCR & AI Parsing
Employees can upload bill/invoice images or screenshots via mobile app or WhatsApp bot
AI extracts: GST number, merchant name, tax amount, invoice total, expense category
Extracted data creates a finance-ready expense record attached to the transaction
Confidence score displayed; low-confidence extractions flagged for human review
FR-04: Smart Approval Workflows
Configurable approval rules: amount threshold, category, merchant type, department
Multi-level approval chains (employee → manager → CFO → founder)
Approvals deliverable via in-app, WhatsApp Business API, Slack app, email
SLA-based escalation: if not approved within X hours, escalate automatically
FR-05: Spend Controls & Policy Engine
Admin can set daily/weekly/monthly limits at company, department, and employee level
Category blocking: restrict spend on personal purchases, alcohol, unapproved categories
Vendor whitelisting/blacklisting by UPI ID or merchant category code
Instant wallet freeze and unfreeze from admin dashboard or mobile app
FR-06: Real-Time Dashboard
Founder view: live company burn, department breakdown, policy violations, pending approvals
Finance view: transaction ledger, GST claims, recurring vendor payments, ERP sync status
Employee view: remaining budget, transaction history, pending approvals, receipt upload
All dashboards update in real time via WebSocket or SSE
FR-07: Accounting Integrations
Bi-directional sync with Zoho Books, Tally, QuickBooks, and generic CSV export
Auto-maps expense categories to chart of accounts
GST-compliant invoice storage with GSTIN, tax breakdowns, and HSN/SAC codes
Webhook support for custom ERP integrations
FR-08: GST & Compliance Automation
Automatic GSTIN lookup and validation via GST portal API
Monthly GSTR-2A reconciliation report (purchases vs. supplier filings)
Missing invoice alerts: flags transactions with no valid tax invoice
Audit-ready PDF export of all expense records with digital signatures

India has approximately 84,000+ DPIIT-recognised startups and over 63 million MSMEs as of 2025. The digitisation wave — accelerated by UPI, GST, and Aadhaar — has created the infrastructure for a modern financial operating layer. Yet corporate spend management remains in the stone age for most sub-500-employee companies. They use WhatsApp groups as an expense management system.
The Indian expense management software market was valued at approximately ₹1,200 Cr in 2024 and is projected to grow at 18% CAGR through 2029, driven by GST compliance pressure, startup proliferation, and remote/hybrid team expansion. The adjacent corporate card and business banking market adds another ₹5,600 Cr TAM.

SpendOS is designed as a cloud-native, multi-tenant SaaS platform built on a microservices-adjacent modular monolith in Phase 1, with a clear migration path to independent microservices as load demands. The core philosophy is: UPI payment rail at the centre, intelligence layer on top, integration layer at the edges.

3.5 Backend Architecture
Runtime: Node.js 20 LTS with TypeScript (Fastify framework for performance)
ORM: Prisma with PostgreSQL for type-safe database access
Queue: BullMQ (Redis-backed) for async jobs; RabbitMQ for inter-service events
Caching: Redis 7 for session store, rate limiting, policy cache, real-time balance cache
File Storage: AWS S3 with pre-signed URLs for receipt uploads
Background Jobs: BullMQ workers for OCR processing, ERP sync, notification dispatch
3.6 Database Architecture
Primary Database: PostgreSQL 16 with pgcrypto for encrypted PII columns
Multi-tenancy: Row-level tenancy with tenant_id on every table + RLS policies
Read Replicas: 2x read replicas behind PgBouncer for dashboard queries
Analytics: ClickHouse (separate cluster) for aggregated spend analytics
Migrations: Prisma Migrate with forward-only migration policy in production
Backup: Daily full backup + continuous WAL archiving to S3 (30-day retention)
3.7 Security Architecture
All PII (name, Aadhaar ref, bank details) encrypted at rest with AES-256 via pgcrypto
TLS 1.3 enforced on all endpoints; HSTS headers; certificate pinning in mobile app
JWT tokens with 15-minute expiry; refresh token rotation with single-use policy
RBAC with five roles: Super Admin, Company Admin, Finance Manager, Manager, Employee
All financial mutations require idempotency key to prevent double-spend
WAF (AWS WAF or Cloudflare) in front of API gateway
Secrets management: AWS Secrets Manager; no secrets in environment variables in production
Penetration testing: quarterly via external vendor; bug bounty programme post-launch

Purpose: Real-time command centre for finance managers
Components: Burn rate chart (7d/30d/MTD), Department spend breakdown (bar chart), Pending approvals count badge, Policy violation feed, GST capture rate gauge, Quick actions bar

5.2 Service Layer
WalletService
Responsibilities: Balance management, allocation, freeze/unfreeze, top-up, transfer between wallets
Methods: createWallet(), allocateFunds(walletId, amount), debitWallet(walletId, amount, txnId), creditWallet(), freezeWallet(), unfreezeWallet(), getBalance()
Business Rules: Debit must be atomic with idempotency key; balance cannot go negative (throw InsufficientFundsError); frozen wallets reject all debits; all mutations logged to wallet_ledger
Error Handling: InsufficientFundsError (400), WalletFrozenError (403), WalletNotFoundError (404), OptimisticLockError (409 — retry with backoff)
TransactionService
Responsibilities: Payment initiation, UPI webhook processing, status management, refunds
Methods: initiatePayment(userId, amount, payeeVpa, category), processWebhook(payload), updateStatus(txnId, status), reverseTransaction(txnId)
Business Rules: Pre-payment policy check required; idempotency key mandatory; webhook signatures verified with HMAC-SHA256; failed payments trigger wallet credit reversal
Error Handling: PolicyViolationError (422), DuplicateTransactionError (409), PaymentGatewayError (502 with retry)
PolicyEngine
Responsibilities: Real-time policy evaluation before every payment, policy CRUD, violation alerting
Methods: evaluatePayment(walletId, amount, merchantVpa, category), createPolicy(), updatePolicy(), getPoliciesForWallet()
Business Rules: Policy hierarchy: company → department → employee (most restrictive wins); policies cached in Redis with 60s TTL; cache invalidation on policy update
Error Handling: PolicyNotFoundError (404), ConflictingPoliciesError (422)
ApprovalService
Responsibilities: Workflow orchestration, SLA tracking, multi-channel notification, escalation
Methods: createApprovalRequest(txnId, approverId), respondToApproval(requestId, decision, reason), escalateOverdue(), getApprovalStatus(requestId)
Business Rules: SLA configured per company (default 4 hours); escalation triggers at 80% SLA; expired approvals escalate to next level; WhatsApp/Slack approval mapped back to request ID via secure token
Error Handling: ApprovalExpiredError (410), UnauthorizedApproverError (403)
OCRService
Responsibilities: Receipt image processing, data extraction, confidence scoring, category suggestion
Methods: processReceipt(s3Key, transactionId), extractGstData(rawText), suggestCategory(merchantName, amount)
Business Rules: Queue-based processing (async); results stored in expenses table; low-confidence (<70%) flags for human review; GSTIN validated against GST portal before storing
Error Handling: InvalidImageError (400), OCRTimeoutError (504), GSTINValidationError (422)

ENGINEERING STANDARDS
10. Engineering Standards
10.1 Coding Standards
Language: TypeScript strict mode (no implicit any, noUncheckedIndexedAccess enabled)
Style: ESLint + Prettier (tabs=2, semicolons, single quotes)
Naming: camelCase for variables/functions, PascalCase for classes/types, SCREAMING_SNAKE for constants
Functions: max 40 lines; single responsibility; pure where possible
Error handling: always explicit; never throw generic Error() — use typed error classes
Logging: structured JSON via pino logger; always include request_id, company_id, user_id
Comments: document 'why' not 'what'; JSDoc on all public service methods
Financial amounts: ALWAYS integers in paise; never use floating point for money
10.2 API Standards
REST conventions: plural nouns, nested for ownership (/companies/:id/wallets)
Versioning: URL-based (/v1/); breaking changes require new major version
Error format: RFC 7807 Problem Detail (type, title, status, detail, instance)
Pagination: cursor-based using created_at + id composite cursor
Idempotency: Idempotency-Key header required on all POST/PUT/PATCH endpoints
Dates: ISO 8601 with timezone (2025-05-15T10:30:00+05:30)
Monetary values: integers in paise; include currency field always
10.3 Git Workflow
Branching strategy: GitHub Flow (main + short-lived feature branches)
Branch naming: feature/SPEND-123-wallet-freeze, fix/SPEND-456-duplicate-txn, hotfix/SPEND-789-auth-bypass
Commit messages: Conventional Commits (feat:, fix:, chore:, docs:, test:, refactor:)
PR requirements: all CI checks green, 2 approvals (1 must be senior engineer), no 'requested changes' outstanding
PR size: max 400 lines changed; split larger PRs
Main branch: protected; direct push forbidden; squash merge only
Release tags: semantic versioning v1.2.3; CHANGELOG.md maintained
10.4 Security Standards
OWASP Top 10 checklist before every release
Dependency scanning: npm audit + Snyk in CI pipeline; no HIGH or CRITICAL unaddressed
Secrets: AWS Secrets Manager only; no secrets in code, .env files, or CI variables in plain text
Input validation: Zod schema validation on ALL external inputs before business logic
SQL injection prevention: Prisma parameterised queries only; raw SQL prohibited
Rate limiting: all public endpoints; authentication endpoints have strict limits
Logging: never log PAN, Aadhaar, full bank account, full card number, or password hash
10.5 Database Standards
All tables have: id (UUID), company_id (FK, multi-tenant), created_at, updated_at
Soft delete: deleted_at TIMESTAMPTZ NULLABLE (not hard deletes for financial data)
All migrations: forward-only; no destructive migrations without 3-sprint deprecation period
Indexes: all FK columns indexed; composite indexes for common query patterns
Transactions: all financial mutations (debit + ledger entry) in a single DB transaction
No stored procedures; business logic in application layer only

11.2 Critical Financial Test Cases
TC-FIN-01: Double Spend Prevention
Scenario: Two concurrent requests with same idempotency key for same amount
Expected: Second request returns 200 with cached first response; balance debited exactly once
Implementation: Redis atomic SET NX with idempotency key TTL of 24 hours
TC-FIN-02: Wallet Freeze During In-Flight Transaction
Scenario: Admin freezes wallet while a UPI payment is in 'pending' status
Expected: Pending payment completes (cannot be reversed mid-flight); subsequent payments blocked
TC-FIN-03: Policy Change During Approval Window
Scenario: Spend limit reduced from ₹5,000 to ₹1,000 while an approval for ₹3,000 is pending
Expected: Approval evaluation uses policy at time of transaction initiation; alert finance manager
TC-FIN-04: Concurrent Balance Debit
Scenario: 10 concurrent requests to debit ₹10,000 each from a wallet with ₹50,000 balance
Expected: Exactly 5 succeed; 5 fail with InsufficientFundsError; balance = ₹0
Implementation: Optimistic locking via version column or SELECT FOR UPDATE
